## About Me

<div class="about-copy">

<p>Greetings! I am Xinzhe Yang (杨欣哲) from Wuxi, China.</p>

<p>Currently I am a PhD student in Materials Science and Engineering at <strong>UT Austin</strong>, under the supervision of <a href="http://sites.utexas.edu/yuanyue-liu">Prof. Yuanyue Liu</a>. Prior to this, I received my master's degree in Materials Physics and Chemistry at <strong>Peking University</strong> in 2025, advised by <a href="http://en.pkusam.cn/">Prof. Feng Pan</a>. I did undergraduate research with <a href="https://www.cheng-group.net/en">Prof. Jun Cheng</a> and obtained my bachelor’s degree in Chemistry at <strong>Xiamen University</strong> in 2022.</p>

<p>My research focuses on the theoretical investigation of electrochemical interfaces, aiming to achieving a comprehensive understanding from multiple perspectives. On one hand, I develop methods to calculate electron transfer rates in electrocatalysts; on the other hand, I simulate interfacial water structures to elucidate their regulation of electrocatalytic reactions. Furthermore, I am interested in applying machine learning methods to accelerate large-scale simulations.</p>

<p>Feel free to reach me via email for discussions, collaborations, or just to say hello.</p>
